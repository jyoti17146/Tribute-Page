About Osho

Osho (1931–1990), also known as Bhagwan Shree Rajneesh, was one of the most original spiritual mystics of the twentieth century. His talks combined Eastern wisdom, Western psychology and sharp humor, inviting people to drop their conditioning and discover their own inner truth.

He created powerful meditation techniques—Dynamic, Kundalini, Nadabrahma and many more— for the modern restless mind. His insights on love, freedom, ego, fear and consciousness are shared in hundreds of books compiled from his discourses.

Life & Spiritual Journey

1931 – Birth in Kuchwada, India

Born on 11 December 1931, Osho grew up questioning beliefs and seeking direct experience rather than second-hand knowledge.

1953 – Enlightenment

At 21, under a Maulshree tree in Jabalpur, he experienced enlightenment—described as a profound disappearance of the ego into existence.

1960–1974 – Talks & Meditation Camps

Traveling across India, Osho gave talks on scriptures, meditation and inner rebellion, leading thousands of meditation camps for seekers.

1974 – Pune Ashram

The Osho Commune (now Osho International Meditation Resort) was founded in Pune, India, becoming a global center for meditation and transformation.

1981–1985 – Rajneeshpuram, USA

A large commune was established in Oregon, USA, as an experiment in conscious living, attracting thousands of sannyasins from around the world.

1990 – Leaving the Body

Osho left his body on 19 January 1990 in Pune, but his meditations and discourses continue to guide seekers globally.

Core Teachings of Osho

Meditation & Awareness

Meditation is not an escape but a way of becoming fully present—watching thoughts, emotions and sensations with clarity and silence.

Love & Freedom

Real love arises from freedom, not possession. When two individuals meet in awareness, love becomes a celebration, not a chain.

Living Totally

Osho invited people to live each moment totally—with intensity, joy and acceptance— turning ordinary life into a meditation.

Dropping the Ego

The ego is a false center created by society. When it dissolves, what remains is pure being—silent, joyful and free.

Rebellion & Responsibility

He encouraged intelligent rebellion—not against people, but against unconscious patterns— so each person can live responsibly and authentically.
